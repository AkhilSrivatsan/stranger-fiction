Every year, I assiduously list down albums I believe to be the best from the year. This exercise serves two purposes. One, to document the music that moved me, both for myself and others. Two, to announce to a faceless audience, here’s how amazing my taste in music is; you should take my taste in music very seriously, take this list very seriously, and on a side note, don’t I have an interesting writing voice? 

For the past few years, I couldn’t articulate that I was making these lists for these reasons, but with age comes self-awareness. Armed with this new self-awareness, I hope to make 2019’s list with a focus entirely on the first, and not at all on the second. Of course, I don’t expect to entirely succeed, but the least I hope for is that I share this music with an open heart, with authenticity. More broadly, I hope that with repeated practice I can write about the things that matter to me with fewer pretensions.
