I guess they’d call [this Mumbai based radio channel I discovered on Radio Garden](https://radio.garden/listen/radio-schizoid/UcH57lai) a mix of psybient and dub and deep house. 

To me it’s part of a subfolder of music I think of as zone-out music, as *B* music, music that I would’ve, back when I’d just started hoarding music on my computer, stored in a folder named *B*. Here’s what my folders were called: 

- *A* (for music that makes you want to jump, so usually dance music) 

- *B* (for music that makes you want to be)

- *Start* (for ‘the classics’)

- *Select* (for ‘the best’)

- *Up* (for uptempo non-dance genres, punk shunk *wagerah*)

- *Down* (for downtempo genres that didn’t just make you want to be, but that you could engage with, trip-hop downtempo *falana*)

- *Left* (‘leftfield’ music, found sounds, noise, etc.)

- *Right *(metal, just metal)

The html file for this radio channel would’ve sat awkwardly in the *B* subfolder of my *Music* folder. 

Although I’d never really ‘gamed’ in my life, I chose a gaming-inspired folder naming system primarily because I was really into [this one Moldy Peaches album](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Moldy_Peaches_(album)) when I came up with it, which had that wonderfully sweet song made popular by *Juno*, a movie I loved. [Anyone Else But You](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ceV62E-c86g). In that, there’s a reference to the Konami Code — up up down down left right left right b a start — which was apparently some sort of all powerful cheat code for Konami games (the only one of which I’ve played is Contra, in which I’ve completed a grand total of two levels). 

Anyway, this Radio Schizoid is a super channel for *B* music, at night, dark room, candles, in the afternoon, lazyboy, blanket, you know the vibe.
